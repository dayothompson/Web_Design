City Latitude vs. Maximum Temperature

At higher latitudes, the Sun’s rays are less direct. The farther an area is from the Equator, the lower its temperature. At the poles, the Sun’s rays are least direct. Much of the area is covered with ice and snow, which reflect a lot of sunlight. Temperatures are lowest here. In summary, Latitude affects the amount of solar radiation a place receives and the amount of solar radiation a place receives is greatest at the Equator and lessens toward the poles. Also, Temperature and Latitude has a strong negative linear association.
